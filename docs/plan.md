## ðŸ§  Why This Approach? (The Science of Learning)

### The Problem with Traditional Reading
- **Forgetting Curve**: We forget 70% of what we read within 24 hours (Ebbinghaus)
- **Passive Consumption**: Reading without interaction = shallow processing
- **No Application**: Knowledge without practice never becomes skill
- **Isolation**: Books read in silos, connections never made

### Our Solution: Evidence-Based Learning Principles

We're applying research from the world's best learning scientists:

| Principle | Source | How We Apply It |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Active Recall** | Roediger & Karpicke | Quizzes, flashcards, self-testing built into every module |
| **Spaced Repetition** | Ebbinghaus, Pimsleur | Review schedules that resurface concepts at optimal intervals |
| **Interleaving** | Bjork & Bjork | Mix concepts from different books to strengthen connections |
| **Elaborative Interrogation** | Pressley et al. | "Why?" and "How?" prompts force deeper processing |
| **Concrete Examples** | Chi et al. | Real-world scenarios and case studies for every concept |
| **Dual Coding** | Paivio | Visual + verbal: diagrams, infographics, animations |
| **Generation Effect** | Slamecka & Graf | You create, not just consume - journals, plans, applications |
| **Desirable Difficulties** | Bjork | Challenges that feel hard but accelerate learning |
| **Emotional Engagement** | Immordino-Yang | Stories, stakes, and personal relevance |
| **Social Learning** | Bandura | Share progress, teach others, community features |

### The Feynman Technique at Core
> "If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."

Every module will ask you to **explain concepts back** in your own words - the ultimate test of understanding.
